Cookie Policy

Last Updated: December 2024

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Cookies help us remember your preferences, understand how you use our website, and improve your browsing experience.

2. How We Use Cookies

Alovre uses cookies for the following purposes:

  • Essential Website Functionality: Enable core features like shopping cart, checkout, and user authentication
  • Performance and Analytics: Understand how visitors use our website and identify areas for improvement
  • Personalization: Remember your preferences and provide a tailored experience
  • Marketing and Advertising: Deliver relevant advertisements and measure campaign effectiveness
  • Security: Protect against fraud and enhance website security

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies (Strictly Necessary)

These cookies are necessary for the website to function and cannot be disabled in our systems. They are usually set in response to actions you take, such as:

  • Adding items to your shopping cart
  • Logging into your account
  • Completing the checkout process
  • Setting privacy preferences

Examples:

  • session_id - Maintains your shopping session
  • cart_token - Stores your shopping cart items
  • auth_token - Keeps you logged in

Duration: Session cookies (deleted when you close your browser) or up to 30 days

3.2 Performance and Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. This helps us improve our website performance and user experience.

Examples:

  • Google Analytics: _ga, _gid, _gat
  • Tracks page views, bounce rates, time on site, and user demographics
  • Provides insights into popular products and pages

Duration: Up to 2 years

Third Party: Google LLC

Privacy Policy: Google Privacy Policy

3.3 Functionality Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization, such as remembering your preferences and settings.

Examples:

  • language_preference - Remembers your preferred language
  • currency_preference - Stores your currency selection
  • newsletter_popup - Remembers if you've closed the newsletter popup

Duration: Up to 1 year

3.4 Marketing and Advertising Cookies

These cookies are used to deliver advertisements that are relevant to you and your interests. They also help us measure the effectiveness of marketing campaigns.

Examples:

  • Facebook Pixel: _fbp, fr
  • Tracks conversions from Facebook ads
  • Enables retargeting of website visitors
  • Builds custom audiences for advertising

Google Ads: _gcl_*

  • Tracks conversions from Google Ads campaigns
  • Enables remarketing to previous visitors

Duration: Up to 90 days for Facebook, up to 90 days for Google Ads

Third Parties: Meta Platforms Inc., Google LLC

Privacy Policies:

  • Facebook Privacy Policy
  • Google Privacy Policy

3.5 Social Media Cookies

These cookies enable you to share content from our website on social media platforms.

Examples:

  • Instagram sharing features
  • Pinterest "Pin it" buttons
  • Facebook "Share" buttons

Duration: Varies by platform

5. How to Manage Cookies

5.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • View and delete cookies
  • Block all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Delete cookies when you close your browser
  • Set exceptions for specific websites

Browser-Specific Instructions:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions

5.2 Opt-Out Tools

You can opt out of specific types of cookies:

  • Google Analytics: Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Facebook Ads: Facebook Ad Preferences
  • Google Ads: Google Ads Settings
  • Network Advertising Initiative: NAI Opt-Out Tool
  • Digital Advertising Alliance: DAA Opt-Out Tool

5.3 Mobile Devices

For mobile devices, you can control advertising identifiers:

  • iOS: Settings → Privacy → Advertising → Limit Ad Tracking
  • Android: Settings → Google → Ads → Opt out of Ads Personalization

6. Impact of Disabling Cookies

Please note that disabling cookies may affect your experience on our website:

  • Essential Cookies: Disabling these will prevent you from shopping on our website
  • Analytics Cookies: We won't be able to improve the website based on your usage patterns
  • Marketing Cookies: You may see less relevant advertisements
  • Functionality Cookies: You may need to re-enter preferences each visit

7. Do Not Track (DNT)

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature. Currently, there is no industry standard for responding to DNT signals. We do not currently respond to DNT signals, but we respect your privacy choices and provide opt-out options as described above.

8. Cookie Duration

Cookies have different lifespans:

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies deleted when you close your browser
  • Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device for a set period (e.g., 30 days, 1 year, 2 years)

9. GDPR and Cookie Consent

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), we comply with GDPR requirements:

  • We obtain your consent before setting non-essential cookies
  • You can withdraw consent at any time through your browser settings
  • Essential cookies are set based on our legitimate interest in providing website functionality
